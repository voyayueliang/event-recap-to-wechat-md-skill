# Moon Content Creation Principles

Use this reference whenever Moon's content workbench turns raw material, field notes, conversations, event records, public research, drafts, or feedback into publishable content. These are base principles, not style decorations.

## Core Thesis

Moon's content system should make Moon more like Moon, not more like a high-output account.

The workbench protects six things:

1. `真实声音`: do not make Moon sound like a generic AI, education, growth, or marketing account.
2. `现场能力`: start from people, scenes, works, conversations, communities, and projects before concepts.
3. `判断密度`: publish one small true judgment rather than many complete-sounding claims.
4. `关系边界`: guests, students, children, families, partners, clients, and community members are not raw material.
5. `精力`: reduce repeated sorting and platform decisions; do not create more mediocre drafts.
6. `长期资产`: every serious output should recover trust, method, case proof, product signal, relationship follow-up, or a next question.

## Human-AI Collaboration

AI is Moon's second desk, not Moon's ghostwriter.

AI can:

- map source material, people, scenes, quotes, facts, and missing context
- compare sources and public reports
- propose routes, structures, title groups, and platform variants
- draft candidate language
- run logic, evidence, voice, boundary, and AI-taste checks
- recover feedback and assets

AI cannot decide:

- what Moon truly thinks
- whether a relationship detail is public
- whether a story should be published
- whether a quote/image/person has permission
- which claim represents Moon
- whether the final piece sounds like Moon
- whether to publish

Treat Moon's "不对劲", "不像我", "太 AI", "不能这么说", and "有点敏感" as hard editorial signals, not minor style feedback.

## Source Before Output

Before writing, build or infer a source map:

- source type: transcript, event, interview, livestream, field note, draft, chat, screenshot, public report, feedback
- time and scene
- people and relationships
- public/private boundary
- strongest scenes, quotes, works, behaviors, and images
- claims already supported
- claims that need confirmation
- likely output route

If the source is messy, sensitive, or too rich, return a content view or material cards before a full draft.

## Expression Track And Experiment Track

Always ask which track the content belongs to.

| Track | Use when | Success looks like | Output bias |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `表达轨` | Moon needs to understand, remember, witness, or articulate something real | clearer self-understanding, trust, resonance, long-term asset | essay, dialogue, field note, conversation digest, reflective post |
| `实验轨` | Moon is testing demand, offer, audience, sample call, workshop, consulting, recruitment, or collaboration | replies, signups, samples, referrals, buyer intent, useful negative signal | small test post, CTA, landing copy, offer note, direct ask |

Expression-track content may stay unresolved. Experiment-track content must name the target reader, hypothesis, minimum test, CTA, and feedback signal.

Do not force expression-track material into conversion copy. Do not bury experiment-track CTAs under poetic reflection.

## Scene Before Concept

Avoid starting from naked trends or broad era statements.

Default order:

1. a real scene, person, work, artifact, quote, or contradiction
2. why it caught Moon
3. what question it opens
4. cautious judgment
5. what remains unresolved or what action follows

Concepts should arrive after readers have seen the thing. If a paragraph could be written without attending the conversation or seeing the scene, it probably needs more material.

## Evidence Beats Fluency

Do not let smoothness hide weak support.

For every strong claim, ask:

- Is it a fact, observation, interpretation, speculation, or public conclusion?
- What source supports it: quote, transcript, scene, screenshot, project, behavior, feedback, public source?
- Is the wording more certain than the material allows?
- Should it be marked `待确认`, weakened, or removed?

Never invent details, causal links, audience reactions, emotional states, results, numbers, or partner positions to make a piece feel complete.

## Anti-AI-Taste

High-risk patterns:

- grand openings: "在 AI 时代...", "如今每个人都..."
- repeated shells: "不是 A，而是 B", "真正重要的是", "本质上", "由此可见"
- every section ending in a neat takeaway
- people becoming examples of a concept too quickly
- stories becoming frameworks too quickly
- fake engagement endings and platform-native hooks that do not fit Moon
- marketing words such as "赋能", "破圈", "闭环", "生态", "链接" when the source did not earn them
- press-release phrasing: "圆满举行", "反响热烈", "干货满满"

Repair by returning to:

- the actual scene
- Moon's question
- original wording
- hesitation or friction
- one image or object
- what is known and what is not known

去 AI 味 is not banned-word replacement. It is restoring human judgment, source texture, and relationship care.

## Dialogue Is A First-Class Output

Do not convert every interview, livestream, podcast, event, or roundtable into Moon's synthesized opinion.

Keep dialogue form when:

- guest wording is stronger than summary
- Moon's questions are part of the value
- the thought is still forming
- the exchange reveals a process
- synthesis would erase uncertainty, disagreement, or relationship texture

Edited dialogue, conversation digest, Q&A, and quote-led cards are not lesser outputs.

## Relationship And Consent Boundaries

Before public output, check:

- names, titles, institutions, and roles
- minors, students, family stories, client cases, sensitive groups
- private chats, screenshots, name tags, whiteboards, addresses, QR codes
- partner, sponsor, employer, or commercial relationships
- direct quotes that may be misread out of context
- photos with recognizable faces or sensitive materials

If unclear, mark `发布前确认`, anonymize, weaken, crop, blur, or keep as internal material.

Do not turn living people into proof points. Preserve relationship context.

## Platform Adaptation

Platform rewriting may change entry, length, density, and format. It must not change Moon's position.

- `公众号`: field texture, dialogue, reflection, public observation, method after scenes.
- `小红书`: sharp concrete entry, saved value, practical distinction, no fake intimacy.
- `视频号`: one scene, one question, spoken rhythm, recordable language.
- `朋友圈`: personal stance, process note, relationship signal, soft invitation.
- `Substack/newsletter`: context-rich field essay or conversation essay for overseas readers.
- `X/Twitter`: one testable observation or thread; do not turn uncertainty into certainty.
- `LinkedIn`: professional clarity without corporate triumphalism.
- `portfolio`: role, method, artifacts, evidence, boundary, case proof.
- `offer/pitch`: buyer pain, result, proof, delivery boundary, next conversation.

Do not expand to all platforms by default. Pick the 1-3 outputs that best serve the material.

## Quality Gates

Before publishing, pass these gates:

| Gate | Question |
| --- | --- |
| `真实门` | Is there a real person, scene, work, conversation, or artifact? |
| `判断门` | What is Moon actually judging, asking, or noticing? |
| `证据门` | Which claims are supported, weak, or unsupported? |
| `声音门` | Does it sound like Moon, or like a polished AI/editorial shell? |
| `边界门` | What is private, sensitive, not consented, or relationship-risky? |
| `行为门` | What real behavior should this invite: reply, sample, booking, referral, saved note, collaboration, or simply memory? |

If a piece fails logic, evidence, voice, or boundary checks, do not polish first. Return to source material, a weaker form, or a confirmation card.

## Feedback And Asset Recovery

Publishing is not the endpoint.

After release, recover:

- reader comments, private messages, repost language, objections, confusion
- replies that show trust, demand, or relationship movement
- sample recommendations, referrals, booking, payment, subscription, collaboration leads
- phrases readers repeat
- questions for the next conversation
- case proof, method notes, templates, offer hypotheses, portfolio material

Distinguish vanity metrics from real behavior. Growth evidence is behavior, not only views.

## Default Output Rule

Prefer the smallest useful output:

- content view before full draft
- card before essay when material is thin
- dialogue before synthesis when the exchange is the value
- weak observation before unsupported conclusion
- platform test before full campaign
- asset recovery before moving on

The workbench should reduce open loops while keeping Moon's judgment alive.
