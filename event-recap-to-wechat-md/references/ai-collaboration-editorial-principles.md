# AI Collaboration And Editorial Principles

Use this reference before drafting and before finalizing a WeChat activity article. The agent's job is to help Moon recover the event's meaning, not to replace her judgment with a polished AI article.

## Collaboration Contract

- AI can organize, compare sources, propose structure, and draft language.
- AI cannot invent the user's stance, emotional response, field judgment, partnership meaning, or public claim.
- When source material is thin, draft lightly and mark what needs confirmation.
- When the user's口述 contains hesitation, discomfort, or uncertainty, preserve it if it is part of the real judgment.
- Do not turn the article into a perfect framework just because the material is messy.
- Prefer "我还在想", "我不太想马上总结", "这可能提醒我" over forced certainty when the source is exploratory.

## Source Hierarchy

Use this hierarchy when sources conflict:

1. User's explicit public boundary and latest instruction.
2. Verifiable event facts: date, location, agenda, speaker names, organizer/partner roles.
3. User's口述复盘 and field memory.
4. Recording, transcript, slides, notes, screenshots, and chat records.
5. Partner-published records, with attribution when their observation or wording is distinctive.
6. AI inference, only when clearly presented as inference or weakened into a question.

Never treat an elegant generated sentence as evidence.

## Evidence Calibration

For each strong claim, classify it before publishing:

| Claim type | Needs support from | Safer handling when support is weak |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fact | agenda, recording, screenshot, official page, partner post | remove specific detail or move to `发布前确认` |
| Observation | scene, photo, audience question, field note | write as "我观察到/现场有一个细节" |
| Interpretation | user's口述, repeated patterns, source comparison | use "这让我想到/也许/对我来说" |
| Partner claim | partner article, quote, public post | attribute, paraphrase, or ask for permission |
| Public conclusion | multiple supported scenes or examples | weaken, narrow scope, or turn into a question |

If a claim is unsupported, remove it, soften it, or mark it for confirmation. Do not patch weak evidence with rhetorical confidence.

## Anti-AI-Taste Rules

Flag and revise these patterns:

- Broad trend opening before any concrete scene appears.
- "这不仅是一次活动", "更是一场...", "赋能", "链接", "共创", "生态", "闭环", "沉淀", "破圈" when the source did not earn them.
- Every section ending with a neat takeaway.
- Numbered frameworks that make the article feel like a course note.
- Smooth motivational lines that could be posted by any education, growth, or brand account.
- Over-explaining what a scene already reveals.
- Turning guests, partners, audience members, children, creators, or communities into proof points for a concept.
- Press-release phrasing such as "圆满举行", "反响热烈", "干货满满" unless the user explicitly wants official copy.
- Overusing "我们看到", "这提醒我们", "值得一提的是", "总的来说".

Prefer these Moon-like moves:

- Start with a small scene, odd detail, sentence, photo, or moment that stuck.
- Let values grow from a showcase, practice, exchange, or friction.
- Keep one or two unanswered questions instead of closing every loop.
- Use concepts only after the reader has seen the thing.
- Let images carry part of the argument.
- Keep relational care visible: people are not raw material.

## Partner And Attribution Boundaries

- Do not silently absorb a partner's distinctive framing as the user's own.
- Attribute partner-published observations when the wording, data, or angle is recognizably theirs.
- Do not imply co-hosting, endorsement, sponsorship, or strategic alignment beyond the source.
- When thanking partners, name concrete contributions when known; avoid generic ceremonial thanks.
- If partner material conflicts with the user's口述, do not smooth the conflict away. Mark it in `发布前确认`.

## Image And Privacy Boundaries

- Do not use audience close-ups, private chat screenshots, children, client materials, name tags, whiteboards, or sensitive slides unless permission is clear.
- If permission is unclear, keep the placeholder and add a `发布前确认` item.
- Do not infer what a photo shows unless the user or metadata says so.
- Captions should explain the image's narrative job, not create a false event moment.

## Final Pass

Before returning the final Markdown, run this compact internal check:

```markdown
## 去 AI 味与谨慎发布检查

- 哪些句子太像通用判断:
- 哪些地方先有概念后有场景:
- 哪些段落太完整、太顺、太像总结:
- 哪些判断缺少材料支撑:
- 哪些伙伴内容需要署名或改成转述:
- 哪些图片/人物/机构信息需要确认:
- 哪些结论应该改成问题、观察或弱判断:
- 哪些真实细节应该往前放:
```

Do not include this checklist in the final article unless the user asks to see the editorial audit. Surface only unresolved issues under `发布前确认`.
